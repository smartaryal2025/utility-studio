The Privacy Risks of Free QR Code Generators

By Kishor Aryal | Developer, Utility Studio

QR codes are incredibly convenient. If you want guests to connect to your home network easily, you generate a Wi-Fi QR code. If you are going to a networking event, you generate a vCard QR code with your personal phone number and email.

To do this, most people type their exact network passwords and private contact details into the first "Free QR Generator" they find on Google. This is a severe security vulnerability.

You Are Uploading Your Passwords

Cloud-based QR generators process your inputs on remote servers. When you type your Wi-Fi password into their text box, you are transmitting that string of text across the internet to a database you do not own. Data brokers can easily intercept and log these credentials.

Furthermore, many of these "free" sites use dynamic QR codes. Instead of directly encoding your data, they encode a tracking link that redirects to your data. They track who scans your code, where they are located, and eventually, they break the link and demand a monthly subscription to reactivate your code.
