The Idea that Files are "Deleted Immediately" is Not True

Most of these cloud based PDF sites have a badge that says your files are deleted after one hour.. Who actually checks that this is done? As a web developer I know how these platforms work.

When you upload a file it does not just stay on one drive. It goes through networks is stored in cloud storage temporarily and is often processed by third party applications. For a lawyer working on a merger an accountant reviewing tax forms or a resources professional handling employment contracts this is a big problem.

I Created a Tool Where Data Theft's Not Possible

I built the Utility Studio PDF Toolkit because I was tired of taking a risk with security. I did not want to build a tool that just promised to keep things private. I wanted one where it was impossible for data to be stolen.

The solution is that the tool works completely offline on the client side.

How Client Side PDF Processing Works

Instead of using a remote server to do the work Utility Studio uses modern browser technology. When you open our PDF Toolkit the entire editing engine downloads, to your browsers storage.

  • There are zero network requests. When you click "Crop" or "Merge" your computers processor does the work. Your internet connection does not even notice.
  • We do not have a database. We literally do not own a server to store your documents on.
  • The tool even works in airplane mode. Once the page loads you can turn off your Wi Fi turn on airplane mode and the tool still works.

If you are working with code, financial records or legal documents you cannot afford to trust that your files will be deleted in one hour. You need to work on your files. Stop uploading your documents to the cloud just to make a change.
